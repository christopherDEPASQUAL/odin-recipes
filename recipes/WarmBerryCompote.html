Warm Berry Compote

Description

Warm Berry Compote is a delicious and easy-to-make dessert that is perfect for any occasion. It is made with fresh berries, sugar, and a touch of lemon juice, and can be served warm or cold. The compote is a great way to use up any leftover berries, and can be enjoyed on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or ice cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries, etc )
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Gather your ingredients: 2 cups mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries), 1/4 cup sugar, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.
  2. Wash and prepare the berries. Hull and slice the strawberries if using.
  3. In a medium saucepan, combine the berries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  4. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the berries start to release their juices, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Once the mixture comes to a simmer, reduce the heat to low. Continue to cook, stirring occasionally, until the berries are soft and the compote has thickened slightly, about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  7. Allow the compote to cool slightly before serving. It can be served warm or at room temperature.
  8. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. Reheat gently before serving if desired.
